About

What is a bobbin?

When I was little, both of my parents went grad school, and I stayed with my grandparents often. Grandma operated a foot pedal sewing machine and made clothes, curtains, and even seat cushions for everyone when she wasn't overseeing the county's trading company during the day! I found her jar of buttons one day, and she taught me how to sew. Bobbins hold not only threads, but know-how that amplified the production of textiles. Likewise, culture is wound, and we weave worn stories into new ones.

Bio

Made in China, reared in Texas. East Coast schooled, West Coast worked. I have been to Burning Man, and I read the Bible. I like EVERYTHING, and I try to see Christ in EVERYONE.

My name is Xi. I currently work as an engineer on data sovereignty projects in the Web3 domain. Heritage belongs to the world, but data is wed to the person. Freedom and trust during our lifetimes empower us to visit Dante's Fixed Stars (Faith, Hope, and Love).

Once upon a time, I studied cultural anthropology, and I still love culture shock. I treasure my time spent on that small town ranch in rural Texas as much as Istanbul and Berlin. I hang on to recipes and probably swiped your hometown's dance moves when I was there.
